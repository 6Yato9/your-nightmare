You are not lost. That is the first thing the forest wants you to believe. The path is still there, you can see it, you are following it correctly, you will reach the village soon. This is what the Leshy does — it does not move you, it moves the forest. The path you are following is a path, but it is not the path you started on, and it leads not to the village but back to the same birch grove you passed an hour ago, the same leaning oak, the same stream that should be east of the road but is somehow west of it now. You have been walking for four hours and you are in the same place.

The Leshy — the wood-lord — is the master of the Russian and Ukrainian forest, a spirit of enormous power who appears as a tall peasant, as a whirlwind of leaves, as a shepherd driving animals, as your neighbor or your grandfather, as a pillar of shadow at the treeline. It has no shadow of its own. Its left boot is on its right foot and its right coat is lapped over its left. These details, if you notice them, confirm what you may already suspect. But noticing details requires the presence of mind that the Leshy specifically works against, which is why most people who encounter it are too disoriented by the time they have the information to use it.

It is not malevolent in the way that something that eats people is malevolent. It prefers to play. It steals cattle and hides them in hollow trees. It makes children fall asleep in inconvenient places. It leads hunters so deep into its territory that when they finally stop and take stock, they have no idea which direction contains anything human. Some of these hunters are never found. The majority emerge from the forest days later, confused and hollow-eyed, saying they were guided by a figure who kept walking just ahead of them, always visible, never close.

The countermeasure is specific and grounded in the logic of reversal — the Leshy works through inversions, and inversions undo it. Turn your coat inside out and put it on backwards. Put your left shoe on your right foot. Sit on a stump and say the Lord's Prayer backwards. These gestures signal that you know the rules and have, temporarily, mastered them. The Leshy finds this less amusing than your bewilderment and releases you. You will find the path immediately. You will reach the village. But the forest will be watching you leave, and the Leshy has a very good memory.
