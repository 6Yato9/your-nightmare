It came at the end of every year, which in the Chinese lunar calendar corresponds to what is now the New Year period. The Nian — whose name means year, or perhaps was the name and became the word — emerged from the sea or from the mountains depending on which account you read, but always at the same time: the last night of the year, when the old year was ending and the new had not yet arrived. The liminal time. It descended on villages in the dark and ate what it found: crops, livestock, people, and especially children. Every year it came and every year the villages were emptied out in advance, people retreating to the hills to wait for the creature to pass.

This went on until an old man, or in some accounts a god disguised as an old man, revealed what the Nian feared: loud sounds, bright lights, and the color red. The crackling of fire. The crack of bamboo split by heat. Anything red hung at doors. These things terrified the Nian completely. The first village that tried this found that the creature refused to enter, backed away from the noise and the color, and eventually fled back to wherever it had come from.

What the Nian story encodes is the structure of the Chinese New Year celebration itself. The fireworks are the noise that drove away the Nian. The red envelopes, the red lanterns, the red decorations everywhere during the festival — all of them are, at their origin, the defense that worked. The celebration is a reenactment of the first victory, the first time humans found that the thing which had terrorized them had a weakness, and exploited it. The Nian had come every year without anything changing, and then something changed, and after that first change the tradition was established and the celebration confirmed the defense every year going forward.

What happened to the Nian is unclear. Some accounts say a monk or deity eventually captured it and bound it. Others say it simply stopped coming. The most interesting accounts suggest that the celebration itself is what keeps it away — that the noise and the red and the fire during the New Year period is a genuine magical operation still performed every year, and that what would happen if it were stopped is not something anyone wants to find out.
